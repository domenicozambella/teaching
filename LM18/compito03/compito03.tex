\begin{exercise}
The language contains only the binary relations $<$ and $e$. The theory $T_0$ says that $<$ is a strict linear order and that $e$ is an equivalence relation. Let $\M$ consists of models of $T_0$ and partial isomorphisms.  Do rich models exist? Can we axiomatize their theory? Is it $\omega$-categorical?
\end{exercise}

\begin{exercise}
In the language of graphs let $T_0$ say that there are no cycles (equivalently, there is at most one path between any two nodes). In combinatorics these graphs are called \textit{forests}, their connected components are called  \textit{trees}. Let $\M$ consists of models of $T_0$ and partial isomorphisms. Do rich models exist? Can we axiomatize their theory? Is it $\omega$-categorical?
\end{exercise}
